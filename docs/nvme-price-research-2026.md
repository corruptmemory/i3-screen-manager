Research conducted 2026-03-28 via Perplexity deep research.

Prices nearly doubled over 16 weeks — worst NAND shortage since 2021-22, driven by AI/datacenter demand cannibalizing consumer supply.

## Price Per Terabyte

- 2024 norm: ~$40/TB
- Mar 2026: ~$100+/TB (150%+ increase)

## Root Causes

- AI/datacenter customers getting 40-60% of NAND production priority
- Samsung pushed 20-30% wholesale price hikes in supply negotiations
- NAND wafer spot prices up 17% in Nov 2025 alone
- Contract prices up 33-60% Q1 2026
- Consumer inventory depleted at major retailers by late March
- Client SSD contract prices rose 40%+ QoQ in Q1 2026 (largest rise of all NAND categories)

## Retailer Snapshot (Late Mar 2026)

- **Best Buy**: $160 – $230 for mainstream 1TB drives
- **Newegg**: $145 – $200 mainstream, wider range with 3rd-party sellers
- Many popular models showing out-of-stock

## Outlook

- **Q2 2026**: Plateau, possible modest further increases
- **Q3-Q4 2026**: 10-15% relief as Samsung P4 line + Micron expanded capacity comes online
- **New baseline**: $150-$200 for 1TB — unlikely to return to 2024 norms
